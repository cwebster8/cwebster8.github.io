The Fibonacci Sequence

The Fibonacci Sequence / 2009 / 12 mins / SD

The Fibonacci Sequence is a self-reflexive mathematical sequence of numbers that, if visualized, creates a spiral. This attempt at filming the sequence creates its own self-reflexive, in which various characters, all played by the same person, vie for a dominant point of view.

Heck

Heck / 2011 / 6 Mins / SD and 16mm

What the Heck Fest was a music festival in Anacortes, Washington from 2002-2011. It was a small summer festival organized by local musicians, coinciding with Shipwrecked Days--a rummage sale that takes over the downtown of Anacortes. This film documents the 10th and final version of What the Heck Fest.

I used a 16mm Bolex, which stopped working after a day or two. For the rest of it, I used a tiny black Asian camera that looks like a jelly bean. Similar image quality, strangely enough. No sync sound recordings. Sound designed by Andrew Ritchey. Filmed in Anacortes, Washington.

The Woman in the Cabinet

The Woman in the Cabinet is a feature film project about an elderly homeless Japanese woman who moves into a man's house and begins living there without him knowing. The film explores their experiences living in this way.

The project is currently in pre-production. Here are some cool materials:

Craig Webster was born and raised in Tacoma, Washington. He studied math and physics in college and holds an M.Sc. from Oxford and an MFA in film/video production at University of Iowa. His films explore fantasies and dreams and in-between spaces and have played at the Edinburgh, Ann Arbor, Buenos Aires, Athens, and Brooklyn Film Festivals. He currently lives and works in Brooklyn, NY.
